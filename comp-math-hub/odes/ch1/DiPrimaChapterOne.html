    zero_index = np.where(dy == 0) # get index of zero elements

    plt.axhline(y=equilibrium_solution[0], color='#f00', linestyle='-')

Definitions¶

  • general solution: all possible solutions of a differential equation

  • integral curves: the family of curves that represent the geometrical representation of the general solution

  • initial condition: a constant that the solution of an ODE equates to when the only variable of the solution to the ODE is set to 0: $\mathbf{p}(0)$
  • initial value problem: when we need to solve an ODE along with an initial condition: $\mathbf{p}(0)$

1.3¶

Definitions¶

The most importnat classification is whether the function depends on a single variable or on several variables. In the case of single variables, we get ordinary diffrential equations, whereas in the case of several variables we get a partial diffrential equations.

$$ \\ $$

• The ordinary diffrential equation $\mathbf{F}(t, y, y^{'}, \dots, y^{n}) = 0$ is said to be linear if $\mathbf{F}$ is a linear function of the variables $y, y^{'}, \dots, y^{n}$. Hence the general ode is of the form $a_0(t)y^{(n)} + a_1(t)y^{(n-1)} + \dots + a_n(t)y = g(t)$

$$ \\ $$

• An ODE is non-linear if is not of the form $a_0(t)y^{(n)} + a_1(t)y^{(n-1)} + \dots + a_n(t)y = g(t)$.

Characteristics of non-linear ODEs

  1. If an ODE has any term such that y or any of its derivatives is multiplied by y or any of its derivatives it is non linear.
    1. If an ODE has any term such that y or any of its derivatives is multiplied by the free variable x, it is non-linear.
$$ \\ $$

• Lotka-Volterra model: a system of equation used in ecological modeling: $$\frac{dx}{dt} = ax -\alpha xy \\ \frac{dy}{dt} = -cy -\gamma xy$$ $$ \\ $$ $x(t)$ and $y(t)$ are the respective populations of the prey and predator species.

$$ \\ $$

• The order of a diffrential equation is the order of the highest derivative that appear in the equation. e.g. $\mathbf{F}[t, u(t), u^{'}(t), \dots, u^{n}(t)] = 0$ is an ordinary differential equation of the nth order. It is convinient in ODEs to write y for $u(t)$, impliying $y^{'} = u^{'}(t)$

$$ \\ $$

• linearization: the process of approximating a nonlinear equation by a linear one

$$ \\ $$

A solution of the ODE $ y^{n} = f(t, y, y^{'}, \dots, y^{n-1})$ on the interval $ \alpha < t < \beta$ is a function $\phi$ such that $\phi^{'}, \phi^{''}, \dots, \phi^{n}$ exist and satisfy $\phi^{n}(t) = \mathbf{f}[t, \phi(t),\phi^{'}(t), \dots, \phi^{n}(t)]$
